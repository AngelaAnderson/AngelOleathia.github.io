Busy Bus

  • ANGEL OLEATHIA

Role: UX Designer

Client: Bloc Project

Summary

For this design exercise, I was tasked with creating a mobile application called BusyBus that would provide public transit users with key information pertaining to their local city transit system. As part of this project, I conducted a user survey using Google forms and created a high fidelity mock-up using Figma.

Problem

Due to expansion, numerous bus routes added and many of those routes stop at the same bus stop. Consequently, bus riders need to know what the next arriving bus is and how much time they have to get to the bus stop.

User Survey Results

17.4% reported that they’re very likely to ride the public bus and 21.7% states they are somewhat likely

Route schedules, online trip planners, and tracking tools are most important amongst respondents

21.7% of respondents often track the location /ETA of arriving buses while 43.5% of users sometimes do

39.1% of respondents are somewhat satisfied with their city’s public transportation system, while 34.8% reported either not having or having a neutral opinion

How do you primarily use cloud storage?

User Stories

  • I want to quickly access the complete transit schedule.

  • I want to know how much time is left until my bus arrives.

  • I want to know what bus is next to arrive and when.

  • I want to be informed of delays in the transit schedule.

  • I want to know my estimated time of arrival (ETA).

Paper Prototype

While testing my three-screen paper prototype with three participants, I found that, as predicted, some features were more intuitive than others. For example, two-out-of-three participants easily identified the ‘Plan Trip’ button when instructed to launch the trip planner, however, none of the participants understood the purpose of the ‘Quick Links’ feature. So, I went back to the drawing board and created a more simple and straightforward design. My second paper prototype is shown below.

HiFi Mock-Up

Changes made to achieve my final mock-up included expanding the background picture and making it appear more subtle by adding an overlay with a higher opacity, changing the navigation menu to better follow the iOS Guidelines, and addeding text captions under the menu icons. Doing so made the design overall more intuitive and easy visually appealing.

Conclusion

BusyBus was the first project I completed as part of Bloc’s coding bootcamp. From this assignment, I learned how to utilize user research in a meaning way to create an everyday solution for communicating public transit information to users. After reciving feedback from prototype testing and helpful tips from Bloc’s grading team, I also picked up some helpful tips on how to keep a desing simple and asetheticly appealing while communicating necessary information.
